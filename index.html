What makes a great skateboard?


Skateboard technology has advanced rapidly since the sport's inception in the early 1950s. Skateboard "decks" are the wooden boards on which the rider stands. The rider attaches "trucks" to their deck, allowing the board to pivot, followed by wheels and bearings that allow the skateboard to roll. Skateboard decks used to be formed of a single solid and flat wood board, but they are now an engineered wood product that is pressed into shape and held together with adhesive.


What Makes a Great Skateboard

Quality Materials

A great skateboard starts with quality materials. Look for boards made from sturdy maple wood or other durable materials. The deck should be strong enough to withstand the rigors of skateboarding.

Proper Size and Shape

The size and shape of the skateboard are important factors in its performance. Choose a board that suits your riding style and preferences. Whether you prefer a wider deck for stability or a narrower one for tricks, make sure it feels comfortable under your feet.

Smooth Wheels and Bearings

Smooth wheels and bearings are essential for a smooth ride. High-quality wheels made from durable polyurethane and precision bearings ensure optimal performance and control.

Sturdy Trucks

The trucks are the metal T-shaped pieces that attach the wheels to the deck. Sturdy trucks made from durable materials like aluminum or steel provide stability and control while riding.

Personal Preference

Ultimately, what makes a great skateboard is personal preference. Experiment with different setups to find the perfect combination of components for your riding style and skill level.
